Find the right way to buy

When to buy from a DfE recommended deal or a framework agreement and when to get bids or quotes.

Always follow your school’s procurement rules. These may be your school’s own rules, or set by the local authority.

Check the different ways to buy and see what fits your situation.

DfE recommended deals

If you can get what you want through one of our deals for schools we recommend that you buy in that way.

The deals have been assessed by the Department for Education (DfE) so that they:

  • comply with buying regulations
  • are easy to use
  • provide things that schools need
  • give you value for money

If you cannot find what you’re looking for, consider using a framework agreement.

Framework agreements

Frameworks are a type of contract you can use to buy goods, works or services for your school.

They are generally easier to use than running a full buying process, so use them if they can supply what you need. Each framework agreement has:

  • details of the products and services available
  • an agreed pricing structure
  • details of the suppliers
  • agreed terms and conditions
  • been checked so they comply with the law
  • been through a full competitive tender process

Each framework tells you how to choose a supplier and place an order.

You will have 2 options:

  1. Select the supplier from a list.
  2. Run a 'mini-competition' – this is where you ask all suppliers on the list to send you a bid and you choose the best one.

Getting quotes from suppliers

Buy this way if:

  • you cannot get what you need through one of our recommended deals for schools or a framework agreement
  • what you are buying is low to medium value

Check your school’s procurement rules to see what they class as low to medium value. We generally say:

  • low is under £10,000
  • medium is £10,000 to £40,000

Guidance: buying low to medium cost things

Advertise a contract and run a buying process

You must run your own buying process, inviting suppliers to submit bids if:

  • you cannot get what you need through one of our recommended deals for schools or a framework agreement
  • you are buying high-value things

Your school’s procurement rules will set what class as high. We generally say high is over 40,000.

However, if what you are buying is over £181,302 you may have to run an EU compliant buying process.

Guidance: buying high value things under the EU procurement threshold.

EU compliant buying process

You need to run this kind of process if:

  • you cannot get what you need through one of our recommended deals for schools or a framework agreement
  • you are buying something over the EU procurement thresholds

The thresholds are:

  • goods – £181,302
  • works – £4,551,413
  • most services – £181,302

Some services for education, health and care are covered by the ‘light touch regime’.

The threshold for these is £615,278.

Check the list of all the services covered by the light touch regime in the legislation.

Guidance: buying things over the EU procurement threshold.

We recommend that you get legal advice if what you are buying is over, or near to, the thresholds
